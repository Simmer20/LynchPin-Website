Sales Strategy & Sales Enablement

Signvrse had developed an innovative accessibility platform backed by a passionate team. As the business prepared for growth, its commercial execution needed to evolve alongside the product. Read Full Case Study

The Challenge

Signvrse had built an innovative accessibility platform and assembled a passionate, mission-driven team. As the company prepared for growth, it became clear that commercial execution needed to evolve alongside the product.

While individual team members communicated effectively in their own way, there was no shared sales language, no structured sales enablement process, and limited consistency in how the company's value was being presented to potential clients and partners.

The challenge was not the product—it was creating a scalable commercial foundation capable of supporting future growth.

LynchPin's Approach

Rather than introducing generic sales frameworks, LynchPin focused on creating clarity, confidence, and consistency throughout the commercial journey. The engagement combined strategic thinking with hands-on facilitation, ensuring that learning translated directly into execution.

1. Commercial Positioning Framework

Developed a practical messaging framework built around five core questions:

  • What do we do?
  • Why does it matter?
  • Who do we serve?
  • Where do we create value?
  • When should clients engage us?

This became the shared language guiding sales conversations, presentations, and customer engagements.

2. Sales Clarity Workshop

Introduced the Johari Window as a practical sales enablement tool. This helped identify gaps between what the internal team believed customers understood and what customers actually perceived. The exercise created stronger alignment across the team and highlighted opportunities to improve communication.

3. Sales Enablement Training

Designed and facilitated interactive workshops covering:

  • Elevator pitch development
  • Sales storytelling
  • Negotiation techniques
  • Objection handling
  • Live role-playing exercises
  • Customer discovery conversations

The emphasis was on practical application rather than theory, enabling the team to immediately apply new skills.

4. Commercial Structure Review

Beyond sales training, LynchPin assessed the broader commercial structure supporting the team. A key recommendation was the introduction of a dedicated Sales Support function to improve communication, coordination, documentation, and execution as the business scales.

5. Leadership Enablement

Each engagement concluded with leadership-ready outputs including:

  • Session summaries
  • Action plans
  • Strategic recommendations
  • Clear next-step roadmaps

This ensured momentum continued beyond the workshops and enabled leadership to make informed decisions quickly.

Key Outcomes

  • Established a shared commercial language across the team
  • Increased confidence in customer conversations
  • Strengthened negotiation and objection-handling capabilities
  • Improved consistency in sales messaging
  • Created a roadmap for future commercial operations
  • Provided leadership with greater clarity around roles, KPIs, and sales support structures
